Mostar's Old Bridge jumps

In Mostar are a traditional diving competition organized every year at the end of July. It is a tradition for young men from Mostar to jump from the Old Bridge to the Neretva . Since the river is very cold, this is quite a risky endeavor and only the most skilled and prepared jumpers will dare to jump....

Mostar Blues and Rock Festival

Is the first international festival of its kind in Bosnia and Herzegovina. For the past eleven editions, the Mostar Blues & Rock Festival has presented great musical names such as: Ten Years After, Ana Popović, Big Brother & The Holding Company, Sugar Blue, Snowy White, Dr. Feelgood, Tito & Tarantula, Deborah Coleman, Danny Shephard, and from the regional performances: Mothers, Partybreakers, Dado Topic & Telephone Blues Band, Josipa Lisac, Zdenka Kovacicek, Vlatko Stefanovski, Yu Grupa and others.

Mostar Summer Fest

Is a two-day music festival that brings together respectable names of the regional music scene of various urban genres. The festival is conceived as an all-day cultural event with an emphasis on evening performances by bands with the idea of becoming a recognizable event on the regional concert map.

Comedy fesival "Mostarska liska"

Launched by the National Theater in Mostar in 1991, is a significant cultural event.The initiator of the Festival was the director Ahmet Obradović . The first festival was held from 11 - 17 May 1991. Due to the war in Bosnia and Herzegovina, the Festival was not held until 2004 . when he was reanimated on the initiative of the actor of the Mostar National Theater, Sherif Aljić . The "Big Fox" and "Little Fox" awards are presented within the Festival.

The mevlud in Blagaj

The tradition of annual gatherings and annual farewells in Blagaj lasted until 1925, and in the same year her last sheikh died . Since then, the tekke has more or less become just a tourist attraction, but tourism between the two wars has not received enough attention either. However, the tradition of the Blagaj mevlud, colloquially known as the dervish mevlud, was renewed in 1975 by Sheikh Fejzulah ef Hadžibajrić - on behalf of the Tariqat Center, and the Mufti of Mostar h.
