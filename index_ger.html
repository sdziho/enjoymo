Old Bridge

Old Bridge is a famous bridge over the river Neretva in Mostar. It was built between 1557 - 1566, the work of the Turkish builder Hajrudin. After the reconstruction in 2004, the bridge was included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. It is known as the main symbol of Mostar and Herzegovina. Mostar's Old Bridge is a stone bridge built of tenelija stone. The arch is almost 29 meters long and 20 meters high, bent near a lowered semicircle. It is characterized by a thin and elegant shape: the profile of the bridge was so thin and high above the water that it was difficult for many to imagine that such a building could be made of huge stone blocks. The bridge is a bright bright color that changes during the day, depending on the sun.

Biščevića house

Biščevića house, better known as Biščevića corner, is located on the east bank of the Neretva River in Biščevića Street. This old Ottoman house dates from the 17th century. The corner protruding above the Neretva on two very high and boldly placed stone pillars, presents this house as the boldest composed work of residential architecture from the Turkish period in Mostar. The chamber or divanhana has preserved the original Ottoman style, the house abounds in authentic furniture, and the garden is a representative example of the Turkish style of construction. Entrance to the house is 3 KM.

Kajtaz house

Kajtaz house (Gaše Ilića bb) belongs to the city residential architecture of the Turkish era and is the most beautiful residential complex with a house for men and a house for women. High walls protect from the strong sun and prevent views from the outside. It is built of stone and wood. The chambers are covered with valuable rugs, and many books written in Arabic have been preserved. The house is open to visitors.

Karađoz-beg's mosque

Karadjoz-beg's mosque is the largest preserved mosque in Mostar. Apart from the tall elegant minaret, its dome attracts attention. The courtyard with the harem extends to the corners of Karađoz-beg and Fejićeva streets. Karadjozbeg's mosque is also one of the most beautiful mosques in Herzegovina. It was built in 1557 according to the designs of the famous Ottoman architect Sinan.

Muslibegovića house

The National Monument "Muslibegović House" located near the Karađoz-beg Mosque is one of the most monumental monuments of urban housing architecture of the Ottoman period in Bosnia and Herzegovina. The complex consisting of a family house (women's courtyard - haremluk) and a business part (men's courtyard - selamluk) was built in the early 18th century, and the main residential building was reconstructed and extended in 1872 on the model of representative buildings in Istanbul. Unlike residential buildings from the earlier period, it was built in the central system of room layout and has 4 floors. The entrance to the residential part is two-arched with a central pillar in the middle, which reflects the influence of the Mediterranean. The building has preserved its authentic form and woodcut, as well as original objects, writings and exhibits, provides visitors with an insight into the way of life of a rich bey family in past centuries. In addition to visiting and seeing the museum setting, visitors are offered the opportunity to consume traditional drinks and cakes and one part of the complex also offers the opportunity to sleep in the original setting.

Koski Mehmed-pasha mosque

Hundred meters north of the Old Bridge, on the left bank of the Neretva, there is the Koski Mehmed-pasha's mosque, which is like an imperial place of worship. It was built in 1617 and is the only mosque in Mostar in which the original color and decoration of the walls have been preserved. Although it was under UNESCO protection in 1993, it was left without a minaret, and neither its courtyard nor its dome were spared.The notes say that Mehmed Koska is from Mostar and that he built the mosque. An inscription reads above its door: “Mehmed-pasha built this mosque to be in this place for the love of God. The Holy Spirit told her in the chronicles: "The temple of God and the abode of good people" (1618).

The source of river Buna

Water erupts below 200 meters of a vertical cliff, topped by the ruins of the old town of Herceg-Stjepan, the last ruler of Herzegovina, before Ottoman rule. Seeing so much water forming a river on its own, the Turkish sultan was probably impressed by it, so he ordered a beautiful Dervish tekke to be built next to Vrelo Buna , which today is the most mystical place in all of Bosnia and Herzegovina. The dervish tekke in Blagaj represents the perfect unity of nature and architecture as well as the source of Islamic spirituality, because the dervish Sufi brotherhood is best known for its asceticism, as well as for preaching one of the most mystical dimensions of Islam - extreme earthly renunciation, ultimate and irrevocable devotion to faith and execution. religious duties.

Blagaj

Only 12 km from Mostar, on the river Buna, is the magical town of Blagaj, the medieval town of the Hum state from which today's Herzegovina emerged in the middle of the 15th century. From the ruins of the old town or the Tower of Herceg Stjepan (ruler of this area before the arrival of the Ottoman Empire in 1465), which was built in a strategic position, on the cliffs above the source of the river Buna, there is a beautiful panorama of the Neretva valley. the top of these ruins.

Crooked Bridge

The bridge was built by the Turkish architect Ceyvan Ketoda in 1558. It is believed that this bridge was built as a test bridge before the construction of the Old Bridge. The old bridge was completed in 1566, when it acquired the title of the greatest architectural achievement built during the Turkish rule in the Balkans. No matter how many times you have crossed the Old Bridge, every crossing is an exciting experience. This one-arched stone bridge was built on the model of the original bridge that towered over the Neretva four hundred years ago, and which was built according to the project of Hajrudin, a student of one of the greatest Turkish builders, Sinan. The length of the vault is 28.7 meters, or 21 m above the level of the Neretva River. Two towers, Halebija and Tara, were built for the guards of the bridge, which served as a weapons warehouse during the Turkish rule

Clock Tower

Another significant monument of the fertile Ottoman period is the Clock Tower , which is located next to the Museum of Herzegovina . This square tower, which dates from 1630, is 15m high, and oral traditions say that it was built under the patronage of the influential Mrs. Fatima-kaduna Šarić. The famous Ottoman travel writer Evliya eleelebi wrote that the sound of the bell from the clock tower could be heard at a distance of three hours. The tower was damaged in the last war. It was renovated in 1999.

Old bazar

In the center of the Old Town you can enjoy the oldest zone of Mostar - Kujundžiluk (old bazaar), a gracefully paved alley from the middle of the 16th century, which still houses characteristic craft shops and traditional restaurants. In the past, it was the lifeblood of trade throughout the region, and during the Ottoman Empire it numbered more than 500 stores. Despite the changes that come with time, this street has retained its authentic ion zionomy and is characterized by mosques and small shops, and to this day it has preserved some of the characteristic crafts, such as hand-made copper and carpet weaving. Along with the Old Bridge, the main character in the story of the whole neighborhood, the old bazaar is a suggestive cross-section of architecture and, at the same time, everyday life in Mostar, represented by liveliness in shops, restaurants (where you can enjoy excellent traditional cuisine) and crowded houses.

Tara and Helebija towers

The two towers, each located on one side of the bridge, protrude like the architectural branches of the bridge itself and resemble the stone guards crossing the river. The Tara Tower is located on the east coast, is semicircular in shape, and during the Ottoman Empire it served to store ammunition. Today it is the seat of the Old Bridge Museum. In Aleppo, on the west side, there used to be a prison on the lower floors and guards on the upper floors. It was also used as an observation post. Behind Tara, on the left bank of the Neretva, is the Herceguša tower, built in the first half of the 15th century, during the reign of Herceg Stjepan Vukčić Kosača.
